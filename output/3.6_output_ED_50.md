extraction prompt now distinguishes a dedicated effectiveness/commencement statement
(category 1 -- often contingent, e.g. "effective when counterparts have been signed",
"shall take effect when the representatives sign", '"Commencement Date" means the date
of this Agreement') from the preamble/caption date (category 2), and instructs the
model to prefer category 1 when both exist.

ordering fix). New regressions: #22 and #36 -- both the predicted risk mode, where a
dedicated clause exists but CUAD's gold is the preamble date (#22, same calendar date
in both) or a different competing effectiveness sentence (#36). Remaining misses
(#6, 7, 22, 26, 36, 40, 43, 48) are CUAD label inconsistencies or retrieval gaps
(#40, 43, 48), not preamble-vs-clause confusion.
